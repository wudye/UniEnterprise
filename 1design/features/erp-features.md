# ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) Features

| Module | Description |
|:--------|:------------|
| **Procurement** | Complete procurement management system from purchase requisition to supplier payment. Handles vendor management, purchase orders, goods receipt, quality inspection, and supplier evaluation to optimize supply chain operations and reduce costs. |
| **Sales** | Comprehensive sales management covering the entire sales cycle from quotation to delivery. Includes customer orders, pricing management, delivery schedules, sales analysis, and performance tracking to drive revenue growth and customer satisfaction. |
| **Inventory** | Real-time inventory control and warehouse management system. Tracks stock levels across multiple locations, manages stock movements, handles reordering, supports barcode scanning, and provides accurate inventory valuation for operational efficiency. |
| **Product** | Centralized product and service catalog management. Maintains product specifications, bills of materials (BOM), pricing structures, product variants, and lifecycle management to ensure accurate product information across the organization. |
| **Finance** | Integrated financial management system covering accounting, payments, and financial reporting. Includes general ledger, accounts payable/receivable, cost accounting, financial statements, and budget management for comprehensive financial control. |

## Detailed Module Breakdown

### üõí Procurement
- **Vendor Management**: Maintain supplier database with performance ratings and evaluations
- **Purchase Requisitions**: Create and approve internal purchase requests
- **Purchase Orders**: Generate and manage POs with terms and conditions
- **Goods Receipt**: Record incoming shipments and verify against POs
- **Quality Inspection**: Inspect received goods against quality standards
- **Supplier Invoices**: Process and match supplier invoices for payment
- **Procurement Analytics**: Analyze purchasing patterns and supplier performance
- **Reorder Management**: Automated reordering based on inventory thresholds

### üìà Sales
- **Customer Orders**: Process sales orders from quotation to delivery
- **Quotations**: Create and manage price quotes for customers
- **Pricing Management**: Configure price lists, discounts, and promotions
- **Delivery Management**: Schedule and track deliveries to customers
- **Sales Returns**: Handle product returns and credit memos
- **Sales Analysis**: Generate reports on sales performance by product, customer, region
- **Commission Tracking**: Calculate and track sales commissions and incentives
- **Customer Credit**: Manage customer credit limits and payment terms

### üì¶ Inventory
- **Stock Management**: Real-time tracking of inventory levels and locations
- **Warehouse Operations**: Manage multiple warehouses and storage locations
- **Stock Movements**: Track all inventory transactions (receipts, issues, transfers)
- **Stock Taking**: Perform physical inventory counts and reconcile with records
- **Barcode/QR Code**: Support barcode and QR code scanning for operations
- **Reorder Points**: Set automatic reorder points and quantities
- **Batch/Lot Tracking**: Track items by batch or lot numbers for traceability
- **Inventory Valuation**: Calculate inventory value using various methods (FIFO, LIFO, etc.)

### üè≠ Product
- **Product Master**: Maintain comprehensive product database
- **Bills of Materials (BOM)**: Define product structure and components
- **Product Variants**: Manage different product configurations and options
- **Specifications**: Store technical specifications and documentation
- **Pricing Setup**: Configure cost, wholesale, and retail prices
- **Product Lifecycle**: Manage product development, launch, and discontinuation
- **Alternative Products**: Define substitute or alternative products
- **Costing**: Calculate product costs including materials and labor

### üí∞ Finance
- **General Ledger**: Maintain complete accounting ledger with journal entries
- **Accounts Payable**: Track and manage vendor payments and liabilities
- **Accounts Receivable**: Monitor customer payments and outstanding invoices
- **Budget Management**: Create and monitor operational and capital budgets
- **Financial Statements**: Generate balance sheets, income statements, cash flow statements
- **Cost Accounting**: Track and analyze costs across departments and products
- **Fixed Assets**: Manage asset acquisition, depreciation, and disposal
- **Tax Management**: Handle tax calculations, reporting, and compliance
- **Bank Reconciliation**: Reconcile bank statements with accounting records
- **Financial Reporting**: Generate comprehensive financial and management reports
