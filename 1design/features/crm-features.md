# CRM (Customer Relationship Management) Features

| Feature | Description |
|:--------|:------------|
| **Lead Management** | Capture, track, and qualify potential sales leads from various channels. Manage lead information, assign ownership, track status progression, and convert qualified leads to customer records. Includes lead scoring, follow-up reminders, and pipeline visualization to optimize conversion rates. |
| **Customer Management** | Centralized customer database with comprehensive 360-degree customer profiles. Store and manage customer information including contact details, company information, interaction history, preferences, and relationships. Supports customer segmentation, data deduplication, and relationship mapping for personalized engagement. |
| **Opportunity Management** | Track and manage sales opportunities through the entire sales pipeline. Monitor deal stages, probability, expected revenue, and timeframes. Includes pipeline forecasting, stage progression tracking, competitor analysis, and sales team collaboration to close deals efficiently. |
| **Contract Management** | Complete contract lifecycle management from creation to renewal. Generate, approve, sign, and track contracts with automated workflows. Manage contract terms, renewal dates, compliance requirements, and document storage for organized contract administration. |
| **Payment Collection Management** | Track and manage all incoming payments and accounts receivable. Monitor payment schedules, overdue invoices, payment collection records, and cash flow. Supports payment reminders, installment tracking, and revenue reconciliation for financial control. |
| **Product Management** | Manage product and service catalog with pricing, discounts, and inventory. Configure product bundles, pricing tiers, and promotional offers. Track sales performance by product, manage product lifecycle, and maintain accurate product information for sales proposals. |
